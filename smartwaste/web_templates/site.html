About the Project

Redefining Urban
Waste Management

SmartBin is a revolutionary urban waste management system that integrates IoT-driven Smart City technology with biophilic, nature-inspired design. Our mission is to transform the act of waste sorting from a mundane chore into a high-tech cultural experience.

We aim to change the mindset of the modern citizen, fostering a new culture of environmental responsibility by blending the organic beauty of nature with the sleek efficiency of future-city technology. Deploying first in Yerevan, Armenia, SmartBin uses dual OAK-D depth cameras and Google Gemini AI to classify waste in real time into six distinct categories with over 95% accuracy.

Our bins act as "Living Landmarks" in the smart city — using visual cues to reward users and signal that they are participating in a global movement of sustainability. By using materials and colors that feel both biological and digital, we bridge the gap for citizens who are currently unmotivated to sort.

The Modular System

Six Intelligent Sorting Modules

Each module is designed to be visually intuitive yet architecturally sophisticated — elevated, professional tones reflecting a premium urban environment.

Paper
A sophisticated take on traditional fiber and cellulose. Newspapers, cardboard, office paper, and packaging materials — all recognized and sorted with precision.
Aluminum
A metallic, high-tech finish representing industrial circularity. Beverage cans, foil wraps, and metal containers — detected by depth sensors and AI vision.
Organic
A deep, biophilic green representing the bridge to nature. Food scraps, biodegradable packaging, and garden waste — composted for circular sustainability.
Glass
A translucent, crystalline-inspired tone representing clarity. Bottles, jars, and glass containers — identified by their unique transparency signatures.
Plastic
A modern, synthetic-yet-clean hue representing polymer recovery. PET, HDPE, and film plastics — classified by brand-aware AI trained on local products.
Other
The "brain" of the system. Its dynamic, gradient-driven identity adapts based on city-wide data, special collection needs, or real-time system status signals.
